What is Reiki?

Originating in Japan in the early 20th century, Reiki is a form of energy healing. Reiki comes from the Japansese word "rei" meaning universal and "ki" meaning life energy. The idea is that we all have "life force energy" that flows through our bodies. Practitioners act as a channel accepting energy from the universe and either hover thier hands slightly above the client's aura field or by gently placing their hands on the client. The practioner uses specific hand placements for the Reiki to reach all Chakras. The client is then receiving the energy through the practioners hands, which aids in healing by promoting deep relaxation, reducing stress/anxiety and relieving pain.

A Brief History

The most common form of Reiki was developed in 1922 by Mikao Usui (1865-1926) People have practiced Reiki for approximately 2500 years. Reiki tradition holds that Mikao Usui received the vibration of energy called Reiki in 1922 while meditating for 21 days on Mt. Kurama. At this time, it was said he achieved enlightenment.
Some researchers state that Reiki was a natural outgrowth of his spiritual practice and that his healing method (Reiki Ryoho) was a natural process in his spiritual practice. Mikao Usui healing method was developed and practiced as early as 1912. Mikao opened clinics in Tokyo and during his travels throughout Japan, he healed the sick and taught this healing ways. Usui trained twenty Shihans (teachers) prior to his death in 1926.
